Effect of Etch Depth on Design of InP Based Multi-Mode Interferometer


M. A. Mahdian, M. Nikoufard, and F. Soleimannezhad
  • Annual Physics Conference of Iran. Aug. 2017. Qazvin, Iran (In persian)

Abstract:

In this article, an InP-based hybrid plasmonic waveguide (HPWG) is used to design and improve a 1X2 multimode interference power splitter. The etch depth of core (InGaAsP) layer in waveguide structure can vary from zero to 500nm from a shallow etch waveguide to a deeply etch one, respectively. By numerical calculations it is shown that the propagation length of the HPWG can drop about 50% by increasing the etch depth, but will decrease the MMI length about 50nm while the power transmission is still more than 90%. As the power splitters are an essential device in photonic integrated circuits, decreasing the footprint of device with increasing the power transmission can help fulfilling nanoscale photonic integrated circuits.
